The site was originally a reforestation area and is managed as a protected area. According to multiple sources it has been government-managed since about 1912. The local government of Impasug-ong and the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR) collaborate to manage and develop it as an eco-tourism park. The development emphasises balancing forest conservation with recreation: hiking, waterfalls, spring swimming, camping.



LOCATION

  • Cedar is located at Barangay Impalutao, Impasug-ong, Bukidnon.
  • If coming from Cagayan de Oro City: From the Agora Bus Terminal, ride a bus bound for Bukidnon or passing through Impasug-ong, and ask to be dropped off at Impalutao or where the signage for CEDAR is. Travel time is roughly 1Â½ to 2 hours.
  • Entrance is near the highway (Sayre Highway) and then a short walk from the highway into the forest trail.


ACTIVITIES

  • Waterfall trekking/hopping: major waterfalls include Gantungan Falls (~720 m from entrance) , Natigbasan Falls (~780 m), Dila Falls (~960 m).
  • Swimming: there is a man-made spring-fed pool (depth ~6 ft) fed from the natural springs.
  • Camping / nature stay: there are camping grounds under forest cover, good for overnight or multi-day nature stays.
  • Hiking/trail walking: Through forest (man-made & natural) and along the riverbeds.
  • River trekking / canyoneering / rappelling (for more adventure-oriented visitors) are mentioned in several blogs
